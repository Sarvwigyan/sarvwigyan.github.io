Rhenium

Introduction

Rhenium is a chemical element with the symbol Re and atomic number 75. It is a rare, silvery-white metal that is part of the transition metals group. Rhenium is known for its high melting point and is used in various high-temperature applications.

The element was discovered in 1925 by German chemists Walter Noddack, Ida Tacke, and Otto Berg, who isolated it from platinum ores. The name "rhenium" is derived from the Rhine River in Germany.

Isotopes

Rhenium has two stable isotopes, 185Re and 187Re, with 187Re being the more abundant. The average atomic mass of rhenium is approximately 186.207 u. Rhenium-186 (186Re) is a radioactive isotope used in some medical applications.

Properties

Rhenium is a dense, silvery-white metal that is highly resistant to corrosion and oxidation. It has a high melting point and is one of the most heat-resistant metals known.

Physical Properties

Chemical Properties

Rhenium is relatively inert and does not react with air or water at room temperature. It can dissolve in concentrated acids and form various rhenium compounds. Rhenium exhibits oxidation states of +1, +2, +3, +4, +5, and +7, with the +7 state being the most stable.

3. Electrical Contacts

Rhenium is used in electrical contacts and filaments due to its excellent conductivity and resistance to wear.

4. Medical Applications
