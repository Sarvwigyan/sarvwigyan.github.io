Early Life

Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Virat Kohli's journey into the world of cricket began at a tender age. From the bustling streets of Uttam Nagar, where he spent his formative years, his innate passion for the game was evident. Even as a three-year-old, he would pick up a bat and ask his father to bowl to him, a clear sign of the cricketing genius that would later emerge.

His father, Prem Kohli, a criminal lawyer, quickly recognized his son's extraordinary talent. In 1998, he enrolled young Virat in the West Delhi Cricket Academy. Under the meticulous guidance of coach Rajkumar Sharma, Virat diligently honed his skills, displaying an unwavering determination and an insatiable hunger for success that would become hallmarks of his illustrious career.

A pivotal moment in his early life came on December 18, 2006, when his father passed away due to a stroke. Despite this profound personal tragedy, a remarkably resilient 18-year-old Virat chose to play a crucial Ranji Trophy match for Delhi the very next day, scoring 90 runs. This incident is widely regarded as a testament to his immense mental fortitude, deep commitment to the sport, and the early signs of the maturity that would define his approach to cricket.

He continued to impress through the age-group levels, making his way into the U-15 team and later captaining the Delhi U-17 team. His dedication saw him transfer to Saviour Convent School during his ninth grade to further his cricketing career, prioritizing the sport that would eventually make him a global icon.

Career Highlights

U19 World Cup Victory (2008)

Virat Kohli rose to prominence by leading the Indian U19 team to a memorable World Cup triumph in Malaysia. This victory, where he also finished as the third-highest run-getter with 235 runs in 6 matches, showcased his early leadership qualities and batting prowess, paving his way for international cricket.

ODI Debut & Rapid Ascent (2008-2011)

He made his One Day International (ODI) debut on August 18, 2008, against Sri Lanka at the age of 19. His consistent performances, including his maiden ODI century against Sri Lanka in 2009, quickly cemented his place. He was a crucial member of the 2011 ODI World Cup winning squad, scoring a century on his World Cup debut against Bangladesh.

Test Debut & Captaincy Era (2011-2022)

Kohli received his coveted Test cap on June 20, 2011, against West Indies. He went on to become India's most successful Test captain, leading the team to 40 victories out of 68 matches. Under his leadership, India achieved historic overseas victories and maintained a dominant run at the top of the ICC Test rankings.

T20 World Cup Champion (2024)

A recent and significant achievement saw him as a key member of the Indian team that clinched the ICC T20 World Cup in 2024. He was also adjudged the Player of the Match in the final for his match-winning knock of 76 runs off 59 balls, adding another major ICC trophy to his illustrious cabinet.

The Chase Master

Renowned globally for his exceptional ability to perform under pressure, especially in run-chases in limited-overs cricket. His calculated aggression and ability to anchor an innings while accelerating at will have earned him the moniker "The Chase Master".

Unmatched Consistency Across Formats

His career is defined by an incredible level of consistency across all three formats of the game. He has maintained high batting averages and scored centuries regularly against all opposition, showcasing his adaptability and elite skill set. He is the only batter to earn 900 rating points in all three formats.

Records & Awards

Key Batting Records

  • Most ODI Centuries: Holds the record for the most centuries in One Day Internationals, surpassing Sachin Tendulkar with 51+ hundreds.
  • Most Runs in T20Is: He is the highest run-scorer in Men's Twenty20 Internationals with over 4,188 runs.
  • Fastest to ODI Milestones: Fastest to reach 8,000 (175 innings), 9,000 (194 innings), 10,000 (205 innings), 11,000 (222 innings), 12,000 (242 innings), and 13,000 (267 innings) ODI runs.
  • Most Successful Indian Test Captain: Led India to a record 40 Test victories out of 68 matches.
  • First Player to Win All Four ICC Titles: Achieved the unique feat of winning the U19 World Cup (2008), ODI World Cup (2011), Champions Trophy (2013), and T20 World Cup (2024).
  • Most Player of the Series Awards: Holds the record with 21 awards across all formats (11 ODI, 7 T20, 3 Test).
  • Most Runs in a Single ODI World Cup Edition: Scored a staggering 765 runs in the 2023 ODI World Cup.
  • Most ODI Centuries while Chasing: An incredible 27 centuries in successful ODI run-chases.
  • Highest Run-scorer in IPL: Leads the Indian Premier League with over 7,263 runs.
  • Only Player to Play 300 ODIs, 100 Tests, and 100 T20Is: A unique record showcasing his longevity and versatility across formats.

Prestigious Awards

  • Arjuna Award: Conferred in 2013, recognizing his outstanding achievements in sports.
  • Padma Shri: Awarded in 2017, India's fourth-highest civilian award.
  • Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award: India's highest sporting honor, received in 2018.
  • ICC Awards Clean Sweep (2018): Became the first player to win the ICC ODI Player of the Year, ICC Test Player of the Year, and Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy (ICC Cricketer of the Year) in a single year.
  • Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy (ICC Cricketer of the Year): Awarded in 2017, 2018, 2020. Also named ICC Cricketer of the Decade (2011-2020).
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year: Multiple times: 2012, 2017, 2018, 2023.
  • ICC Test Player of the Year: Received the award in 2018.
  • ICC Spirit of Cricket Award: Honored in 2019.
  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World: Recognized in 2017, 2018, 2019.
